## Combat Execution: Rotation, Positioning, and Common Mistakes

The gap between average and strong revolver players is mostly execution, not stats.

### Practical Rotation (Simple and Effective)

1. Open with primary to establish pressure and proc states.  
2. Swap to revolver and apply mark to priority target.  
3. Burst during mark uptime (or fanning window if available).  
4. Swap back before overcommitting in danger range.  
5. Repeat while kiting and managing line-of-sight.

### Mistakes That Hurt Revolver Value

| Mistake | Why It’s Bad | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Holding revolver too long in swarms | You lose AoE tempo | Swap back to primary sooner |
| Ignoring reload rhythm | Misses burst opportunities | Practice timing in range area |
| Marking random trash targets | Wastes damage amp window | Mark elites/boss parts first |
| Building only for raw damage | Feels good on paper, clunky in runs | Balance with reload or draw speed |
| Late target swaps | Mark expires before team focus | Communicate focus target quickly |

> **Tip:** In higher difficulty missions, survival DPS beats dummy DPS. Clean swaps and safe angles will outperform greedy peeking.

### Team Utility Angle

A well-played **Far Far West revolver** helps the whole squad by increasing priority target damage intake. In coordinated groups, that’s often more valuable than one player chasing isolated personal numbers.
