## High-pressure shopping: when to skip the trader entirely

A major skill gap in 2026 is not just buying well, but **skipping correctly**. Sometimes the correct move is to ignore the trader and keep momentum.

Skip conditions:

- You are currently being surrounded.
- Objective timer pressure is active.
- Your health is unstable and disengage path is unsafe.
- You already have enough scaling and only need clean execution.

Take conditions:

- Nearby area is mostly cleared.
- You have planned purchases (not browsing).
- Your current setup has a known weakness the trader can patch.
- You can re-enter objective lane quickly.

| Situation | Interact or Skip? | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Active elite wave + low HP | **Skip** | UI time can get you killed |
| Quiet pocket after clearing event | **Interact** | Safe window, good conversion |
| Payload stalled by enemy density | **Usually skip** | Regain control first |
| Between objectives with escape path | **Interact** | Best low-risk shopping moment |

This single discipline improves completion consistency more than most players expect.

## Common mistakes to fix immediately

Most losses around the **Far Far West wandering trader** come from decision speed, not mechanical skill alone.

### Mistake 1: Shopping without a plan
You open the trader, browse too long, then get punished by respawns.

**Fix:** Enter with a strict shortlist (survive, control, scale).

### Mistake 2: Overvaluing economy during danger
Gold value is real, but dead runs generate nothing.

**Fix:** Stabilize first, then convert extra economy later.

### Mistake 3: Ignoring objective type
A boss burn build may struggle on escort chaos.

**Fix:** Rebuild around your next 2-3 minutes, not your favorite fantasy setup.

### Mistake 4: Buying redundant power
Stacking the same stat beyond practical utility can reduce flexibility.

**Fix:** Patch weaknesses before amplifying strengths again.
