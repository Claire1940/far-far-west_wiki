## Solo vs Squad Strategy for Far Far West cactus locations

You can complete all **Far Far West cactus locations** solo, but the time and risk profile is very different compared to a 3-4 player group.

| Mode | Strengths | Weaknesses | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Solo** | Full route control, no coordination delay | Long walks, high interruption risk | Off-peak lobbies or cautious pacing |
| **Duo** | One carrier + one scout/cover | Limited map split potential | Balanced speed + safety |
| **3-4 Squad** | Split pair responsibilities, fastest clears | Needs communication discipline | Competitive completion timing |

### Solo Carry Playbook

If you’re soloing **Far Far West cactus locations**, use this structure:

- **Pick long routes first** while you’re mentally fresh.
- **Avoid hot gunfire valleys** even if it adds 10-15 seconds.
- **Use terrain breaks** (rock spines, dune crests) instead of straight-line runs.
- **Don’t overcommit in combat.** If you’re carrying and pressured, reposition first.

### Squad Split Method

For squads, designate roles before moving:

- **Carrier A / Carrier B:** each handles a separate side of the map.
- **Scout:** identifies next child position and pings safe route.
- **Anchor/Cover:** holds hostile angles near chokepoints.

A split-carry plan can dramatically cut total mission time, especially when both carriers avoid overlap and only regroup for contested areas.

## Common Mistakes That Slow Down Cactus Runs

Even experienced players lose minutes to small errors. Here are the ones to fix first:

- **Starting in the center** instead of clearing the furthest edge.
- **Fighting every encounter** while carrying.
- **Ignoring elevation safety** and crossing open flats repeatedly.
- **No role assignment** in squads (two players grabbing same child).
- **Map-only navigation** without visual glow scanning.

If you clean up those five, your mission pace usually improves right away.
