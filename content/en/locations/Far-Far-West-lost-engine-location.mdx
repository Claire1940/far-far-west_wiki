## The Far West: Restoring the Great Bell

The journey often begins in the Far West map, specifically at the church located in the southeast quadrant. Here, you will encounter the Churchkeeper, who tasks you with restoring the Great Bell atop the tower. To do this, you must locate three smaller sets of bells hidden throughout the region.

Each bell location triggers a memory-based mini-game. Upon interaction, the bells will ring in a specific sequence. You must shoot the bells in that exact order to succeed. If you fail, simply interact with the floor bell to restart the sequence.

| Bell Location | Mini-game Type | Reward Component |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Southeast Church** | Sequence Shooting | Great Bell Restoration |
| **Northern Outpost** | Sequence Shooting | Bell Part A |
| **Western Ridge** | Sequence Shooting | Bell Part B |

Once all three mini-games are finished, return to the church tower. Interacting with the Great Bell will award you the **Bellshot Joker**. This green-rarity weapon Joker provides a 5% chance to spawn a bell above an enemy. When you shoot this spawned bell, it heals you for 20 health, making it one of the most reliable sustain items in 2026.

## The Canyon: Medallions of the Sheriff

The Canyon secret mission is located in a cave south of the map's center. To unlock the treasures within, you must find four distinct medallions. While the first medallion is easily recovered from the body of the Sheriff, the remaining three require completing specific environmental puzzles.

1.  **The Dig Site (East):** Head under the bridge on the eastern side of the map. Pick up a shovel and look for mounds of dirt covered with small rocks. Dig at these spots until the medallion appears.
2.  **The Demolition Zone (North):** Look for red glowing rocks. Use the nearby dynamite crates to blast through the layers of stone until you uncover Bobb’s Medallion of Luck.
3.  **The Shooting Gallery (West):** In the darker western region, you must find and shoot 10 hidden targets. Once all targets are destroyed, a safe will open containing the final medallion.

> **Warning:** The targets in the western canyon can be difficult to see due to the low lighting. Use a weapon with a tracer or a flashlight attachment to locate them more efficiently.

After inserting all four medallions into the cave slots, you will earn the **Pickp Pick Joker**. This item grants a 5% chance to cause a bleed effect on enemies, dealing damage over time. Additionally, the cave contains a massive haul of gold and the **Legendary Elder Pick**, which significantly increases mining speed.

## Far Far North: The Snowman and the Lost Engine Parts

When players search for the **Far Far West lost engine location**, they are often directed toward the Far Far North map. This region features a cave in the northern section where a dismantled snowman stands. To complete this mission, you must recover three body parts (Head, Chest, and Base) and roll them back to the cave.

While navigating these parts, you must be extremely careful. The physics of the snowballs can cause them to get stuck in trees or rock crevices. If a part becomes immovable, you may need to restart the session to reset its position.

| Snowman Part | Location | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Head** | Near Cave Entrance | Easy |
| **Chest** | Eastern Slope | Medium |
| **Base (The "Engine")** | Southern Valley | Hard (Long Route) |

Completing this arduous task does not grant a Joker, but instead rewards the player with the exclusive **Carriable Skin**, a highly sought-after cosmetic for 2026.

## The Desert: Cactus Reunion

The Desert mission is arguably the most time-consuming. Scattered across the sands are 12 cactuses: six "parents" and six "children." Your goal is to find the small child cactuses and carry them back to their respective parents. 

Each cactus provides a cryptic hint regarding the **Far Far West lost engine location** and the whereabouts of their missing family members. To optimize this run, it is recommended to use a high-mobility mount or character build, as the distance between some pairs is significant.

*   **Reward:** Cactus Day Joker.
*   **Effect:** 3% chance on kill to spawn an explosive cactus mine. These mines can explode multiple times, providing excellent area-of-effect (AoE) damage for clearing mobs.

## Woodlands: The Mushroom Path

In the Woodlands, head to the large cave east of the map's center. Inside, a stone tablet displays a sequence of numbers corresponding to locations on the map. You must visit seven specific mushroom clusters in a clockwise order around the map's perimeter.

Touching the mushrooms in the wrong order will reset your progress. Once all seven are activated, return to the stone tablet to claim the **Shroom Grave Joker**. This Joker has a 10% chance to spawn a healing mushroom on kill. While it heals for 10 health, be aware that it also applies a visual "buzz" effect that can distort your vision for several seconds.

## Area 41: The Alien Monoliths

The final secret mission in Area 41 requires knowledge of all previous maps. You will find alien monoliths with strange symbols. The hint provided mentions "the frontier, the sand, the cliffs, and the trees." This refers to the order in which you must input the symbols found on the monoliths in the Far West, Desert, Canyon, and Woodlands maps.

**Symbol Input Order:**
1.  **Frontier** (Far West)
2.  **Sand** (Desert)
3.  **Cliffs** (Canyon)
4.  **Trees** (Woodlands)

Completing this sequence unlocks the **Anti-Gravity Falls Joker**. This unique item has a 2% chance to spawn a blue anti-gravity orb upon an enemy's death. This orb causes the player to float upwards, which can be useful for reaching high vantage points, though its utility in direct combat is limited compared to the healing Jokers.

> **Tip:** If you are struggling with the height of the anti-gravity jump, pair it with an impulse grenade to gain even more vertical distance.

## FAQ

**Q: Where exactly is the Far Far West lost engine location?**

A: The "lost engine" typically refers to the heavy base part of the snowman in the Far Far North or the mechanical parts found in the Canyon's hidden safe. Check the southern valley of the North map for the most common "lost" component.

**Q: Are the secret mission rewards permanent?**

A: Yes, once you unlock a Joker or a skin from a secret mission, it is added to your collection. However, some items like the Elder Pick may need to be re-acquired in subsequent runs if they are lost during gameplay.

**Q: Which secret mission Joker is the best for bosses?**

A: The **Pickp Pick Joker** from the Canyon is highly recommended for bosses due to its damage-over-time bleed effect. For survivability, the **Bellshot Joker** from the Far West map is superior.

**Q: Can I complete these missions in a group?**

A: Yes, completing these missions with friends can make tasks like rolling the snowman parts or finding the 12 cactuses much faster. Progress usually counts for all players in the immediate vicinity.
