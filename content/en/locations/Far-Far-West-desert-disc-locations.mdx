## Detailed Far Far West Desert Disc Locations

To help you optimize your travel route, we have categorized the locations based on their position on the world map. It is generally most efficient to start at the center and work your way toward the eastern outskirts.

### 1. The Central Oasis Fragment
The first stop on your journey is the most accessible. Located almost exactly in the middle of the desert map, you will find a small, lush oasis. This area serves as a natural landmark against the surrounding sand dunes. The disc fragment is located near the water's edge, often hidden slightly by the desert flora. 

> **💡 Tip:** Use the Oasis as a central hub for resupplying before heading out to the more remote northeastern and eastern locations.

### 2. The Northeast Pyramid Fragment
The second piece of the puzzle is tucked away within the ancient architecture of the desert. Head toward the northeastern side of the map. You are looking for a specific "bulge" or extension on the map layout where several pyramids are situated. 

As you approach the pyramids, listen closely; the music from the disc often becomes audible as you get closer to the correct structure. One of the pyramids contains a small interior chamber or alcove where the fragment is hidden. Unlike the oasis fragment, this one requires a bit of indoor searching, though the "Train Rush" melody will act as a proximity sensor to guide you.

### 3. The Eastern Island Tower Fragment
The final fragment is the most difficult to reach due to its elevation. Travel to the far east side of the desert, where the landscape breaks into a series of small islands. On one of these islands stands a tall, wooden lookout tower.

To claim this fragment, you must reach the very top of the structure. You can use the ladder provided on the side of the tower, or if you have unlocked advanced jump boosts, you can leap between the platforms to reach the summit faster. The fragment sits right on the top floor, overlooking the eastern sea.

## Unlocking the "Train Rush" Track

Once you have successfully visited all the **Far Far West desert disc locations** and retrieved the three fragments, the game will automatically prompt you that the track is complete. Originally titled "Desert," the track was updated to "Train Rush" to better reflect its fast-paced, rhythmic tempo that mimics a locomotive barreling through the frontier.

You can play this track at any jukebox found in the game's towns or within your personal player housing. It is widely considered one of the best tracks for high-intensity gameplay, making the search for these **Far Far West desert disc locations** well worth the effort.

> **Warning:** Be aware that enemies often patrol the area around the pyramids. Ensure you are geared for combat before entering the pyramid structures to claim your second fragment.

## FAQ

**Q: Why is the disc called "Train Rush" if the fragments were labeled "Desert"?**

A: The developers changed the name of the track in a post-launch update. While the fragments you pick up might still be referred to as "Desert fragments" in some legacy menus, the final song added to your collection is "Train Rush."

**Q: Do I need special tools to reach the Far Far West desert disc locations?**

A: No special tools are strictly required, as the tower has a ladder and the pyramid is accessible on foot. However, having mobility boosts makes the process much faster and safer.

**Q: Can I collect the fragments in any order?**

A: Yes, the fragments can be collected in any order. The guide suggests a route from the center to the east for efficiency, but you can start at the islands or pyramids if you prefer.

**Q: What happens if I lose a fragment after picking it up?**

A: Once a fragment is picked up, it is permanently tied to your account progress. Even if you are defeated by enemies before reaching a save point, the fragment usually remains in your collection quest log.
